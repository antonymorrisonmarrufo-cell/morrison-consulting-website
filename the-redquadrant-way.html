Five phases

Every consultancy says they have a methodology. Most of them mean “we have a slide deck.” Ours is different because it has been tested across 100+ council engagements since 2009, refined with every one, and taught to ~2,000 practitioners through the PSTA.

The RedQuadrant Way is not a rigid framework imposed on your organisation. It is a set of principles, tools, and practices that we adapt to fit the reality of your situation.

01

Discover

Before we design anything, we need to understand the system as it actually works — not as the org chart says it should. We use systems thinking, stakeholder interviews, data analysis, and rich pictures to build a shared picture of the current state. This phase surfaces the real problems, not just the symptoms.

02

Design

We design solutions with the people who deliver the service, not for them. Co-design workshops, service blueprints, operating model options, and scenario planning. Every design is tested against practical constraints: budget, politics, capability, and time.

03

Deliver

We do not hand over a report and leave. Our consultants stay in the room for delivery — managing programmes, leading workstreams, unblocking problems, and making sure the design actually works when it meets reality. This is what we mean by “inside governance, not alongside it.”

04

Embed

Change that depends on the consultant is not change. This phase is about embedding new ways of working into the organisation so they survive after we leave. New processes, governance, performance frameworks, and team capabilities.

05

Transfer

The final phase is knowledge transfer. Through the PSTA and structured handover, we make sure your team can maintain, iterate, and improve what has been built. Our goal is to make ourselves unnecessary.

What makes it different

Systems thinking at the core

We do not break problems into parts and optimise each one separately. We look at the whole system — the connections, the feedback loops, the unintended consequences. This is why our solutions stick: they address root causes, not symptoms.

Rich pictures and visual methods

Complex systems are hard to discuss in words alone. Our in-house design team creates rich pictures that make the invisible visible — showing how a service really works, where the blockages are, and what a better future looks like. Used by the LGA, Natural England, Kent County Council, and the Heritage Council of Ireland.

Practitioner-led

Every person on an engagement has done the job before. Former directors, heads of service, and programme directors. They do not need to learn the sector while working on your programme.

Knowledge transfer built in

We do not treat knowledge transfer as a final deliverable. It is built into every phase. Your team works alongside ours from day one. The PSTA provides structured development. By the time we leave, your people can carry the work forward.

Tools and approaches we use

  • Systems thinking — seeing the whole, not just the parts
  • Service design — designing around the user, not the org chart
  • Rich pictures — making complex systems visible and shareable
  • Positive deviance — finding what is already working and scaling it
  • Scenario planning — preparing for multiple futures, not betting on one
  • Lean rapid improvement — quick wins that build momentum
  • Action learning — learning by doing, with structured reflection
  • Programme and project management — governance, delivery, benefits realisation
