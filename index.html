What comes next

What a refined version would add.

This is a first draft. A refined version — the kind you'd want before committing to a build — would add the following. Each step dramatically improves accuracy, confidence, and stakeholder buy-in.

Kickoff workshop with RQ leadership

90 minutes with Benjamin, Natasa, and one or two other decision-makers. Agrees the strategic context, validates the 4 audiences, confirms success measures, captures brand personality and future-state ambitions. Replaces every "best reconstruction" assumption in this first draft with real client input.

Real analytics and behavioural data

Google Analytics, Search Console, any heatmaps or session recordings, tender win/loss data, enquiry volume. Every projection in this first draft uses sector benchmarks as proxy; a refined version anchors every projection to real Red Quadrant baseline metrics.

Brand and personality capture

A proper brand workshop surfaces the tonal anchors, the "looking like us" test, the 5-year future-state ambition. These shape every design decision downstream. Without this, the first draft guesses at brand intent from the existing site.

Real imagery and case study content

Commissioned photography (12 team headshots + environmental shots), real case studies with quantified outcomes gathered from Red Quadrant's recent engagements, named client testimonials with permissions. The biggest single credibility lift.

Mid-point design review

A one-hour session where the four options are walked through at wireframe stage and the client reacts. Catches "that's not us" early, before design decisions calcify. One of the highest-leverage human input moments in the whole process.

Full content population

This first draft populates the homepage with detailed first-draft copy. A refined version would write production-ready copy for all 28 pages in the new IA, with editorial review, plain-English pass, accessibility check, and SEO optimisation.
